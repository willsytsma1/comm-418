# MPD's districts east of the river report stark highs in gun use

Officers from the two D.C. Police districts east of the Anacostia River have been far less gun shy over the past five years, publicly available data show.

From 2020 through 2024, officers in the Metropolitan Police Department's Seventh and Sixth Districts reported pointing or firing their firearms 648 and 501 times, respectively. That's 49 more times than all of MPD's five other districts combined.

In those years, officers in the Seventh and Sixth Districts reported firing their weapons a total of 44 times.

Neighborhoods east of the Anacostia, patrolled by officers of those two districts, are mostly black-majority neighborhoods. Those areas are also comprised of the city's lowest-income areas, according to [median household income data from the District of Columbia Office of Planning](https://planning.dc.gov/sites/default/files/dc/sites/op/page_content/attachments/Key%20Demographic%20Indicators%202019-2023.pdf). That those areas are home to some of the most Black and least wealthy neighborhoods raises questions about potential bias-related discrepancies in how MPD polices different communities.
