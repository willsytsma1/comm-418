# MPD's districts east of the river report stark highs in gun use

Officers from the two D.C. Police districts east of the Anacostia River have been far less gun shy over the most recent five years with publicly available data, use of force data from the Metropolitan Police Department show.

From 2020 through 2024, officers in the MPD's Seventh and Sixth Districts reported pointing or firing their firearms 648 and 501 times, respectively. That's 49 more times than all of MPD's five other districts combined.

In those years, officers in the Seventh and Sixth Districts reported firing their weapons a total of 44 times.

Neighborhoods east of the Anacostia, patrolled by officers of those two districts, are mostly black-majority neighborhoods. Those areas are also comprised of the city's lowest-income areas, according to [median household income data](https://planning.dc.gov/sites/default/files/dc/sites/op/page_content/attachments/Key%20Demographic%20Indicators%202019-2023.pdf) from the District of Columbia Office of Planning. The fact that those areas are home to some of the most Black and least wealthy neighborhoods raises questions about potential bias-related discrepancies in how MPD polices different communities.

Crime rates could also account for the differences between districts. According to Crime Cards published on MPD's website, violent crimes have occured much more frequently over the past five years in the Sixth and Seventh districts than in districts west of the Anacostia. Officers patrolling districts east of the river encounter more scenarios where they need to deploy a firearm to protect themselves within the limits of the use of force framework that MPD sets out for them.

The reporter could not reach MPD for comment in time for publication. Data from last year, 2025, has yet to be published on MPD's website.
