# The Journal's "AMERICANS ARE NOW A TARGET IN TRUMP’S IMMIGRATION CRACKDOWN"
[Access story here](https://www.wsj.com/us-news/immigration-protests-noem-minneapolis-0b8bd496?mod=article_inline).


An investigation that the Wall Street Journal released last dove into the Trump administration's "campaign to detain and demonize" dissenters to its immigration crackdown. They looked at X posts accusing 279 people of assaulting officers, paired with federal assault charge referrals data. They found that few allegations held up to scrutiny.

I appreciate that the Journal dove into the social media aspect of these assault allegations. In my own reporting on assault charges, a piece of what I found was that the amount of publicity that DHS has given to their agents' narratives makes it nearly impossible for the people they accuse to take their narrative off of their image and record. Even after charges were dismissed, DHS has pushed allegations on those folks and kept X posts with agents' unsubstaniated claims online. The Journal looked at the outcomes of cases against 83 of those DHS accused on X and found that most weren't charged (according to their treemap).

I also appreciate the coding and multimedia elements of their piece. I do wonder, though, if they could have taken a few of those assets off of the story. At a certain point, I think videos, images and documents may begin to distract from the writing, without which the evidence's throughlines aren't so apparent.
