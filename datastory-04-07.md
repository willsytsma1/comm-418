# Propublica's "Trump’s Justice Department Dropped 23,000 Criminal Investigations in Shift to Immigration"

Get link [here](https://www.propublica.org/article/trump-doj-immigration-bondi-declinations-criminal-investigations).

I like that the reporters in this story were able to link a trend in the TRAC data to a specific cause. The inclusion of the Trump administration's order and perspectives of attorneys who recieved it added a lot of substance to the issue that the data highlights. I also like that the reporters included data about the number of immmigration cases the DOJ is pursuing in lieu of criminal cases, showing the administration's priorities and how it might contradict Trump's rhetoric about coming down hard on crime. 

I do wish the reporters were more clear about their rationale behind the statement "ProPublica determined the increase is not the result of inheriting a larger caseload or more referrals from law enforcement." The first explanation of this is in the methodology section. To me, we owe readers more explanation about determinations as big as that one.
