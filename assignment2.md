The question I'm choosing to answer with data is: "How does the number of uses of force by Border Patrol agents during the last fiscal year (2025) stack up against years prior."

Part of the reason I chose this question is that I know that *most* law enforcement agencies are required to report use of force data, and often have it publishable on their website. So, I went directly to CBP.gov, and surely enough I found a use of force dashboard under "Assault and Use of Force Statistics." 

A number of factors make the dataset, at least in my view, reliable. The first is that the data are coming directly from the agency. All things equal, the data they're looking at is aggregated from the reports they've recieved from their own officers and agents, meaning that there's minimal room for reported incidents to get lost in upload process. The less the steps, the less room for error, at least in theory. The second is that there are laws and policies mandating CBP to report this data. This doesn't mean it's 100% accurate, but it does mean there's a greater burden on them for accuracy. Individual agents are also required to report their uses of force per a CBP-wide policy, meaning there's a high chance of accuracy at the collection level.
